How do college students feel?
Do they feel things?
Let's find out!

Answered By Nicole Smith, Presented By Peter Krisko

As an attempt to get more into the mind of students, these questions were asked to multiple groups of students.

  • Why do you want to be a designer?
  • What makes you nervous about becoming a designer?
  • What excites you about design?
  • What are your strengths?
  • What are some areas of opportunity to work on?
  • What aspects of graphic design to you gravitate towards?
  • What are the roles and responsibilities of a graphic design in society?
  • When/where/why are you at your most creative/productive?
  • What are your comfort levels with giving and receiving feedback? How might you improve your skills here? What strategies have worked for you?
  • What is a website?

1. Why do you want to be a designer?

To share creativity with others and help with the movement of graphic design but I also like money.

2. What makes you nervous about becoming a designer?

Being told that what I do isn’t good enough and facing struggling challenges.

3. What excites you about design?

Exploring new techniques and learning new abilities that further improve my design skills.

4. What are your strengths?

Pushing myself beyond my boundaries and taking kindly to feedback.

5. What are some areas of opportunity to work on?

Developing new styles and working with finer details, also not procrastinating.

6. What aspects of graphic design to you gravitate towards?

Logo designs and posters. playing around with type is really fun too to express different emotions and shit like that, package design ftw.

7. What are the roles and responsibilities of a graphic design in society?

The creators that draw in viewers through emotions using well crafted elements, colours, type, etc and help establish brands. they basically make the world a more interesting place by displaying messages in either literal or abstract ways that others could never think of .

8. When/where/why are you at your most creative/productive?

I work well under pressure but i prefer working at home in privacy because that’s where i usually produce my best work. it’s tranquil.

9. What are your comfort levels with giving and receiving feedback? How might you improve your skills here? What strategies have worked for you??

Roast me to be honest. Criticism makes the world go around.

10. What is a website?

A platform where strangers can connect, share and display works that can entice the viewer. it can be a social platform, a store, or even a block of information that can be accessed world wide.
